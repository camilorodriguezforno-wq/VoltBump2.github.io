Technology

How It Works

Four simple steps, from the road to the power grid.

01

Vehicle passes over

The vehicle weight (1 to 40 tons) compresses the VoltBump module embedded in the pavement.

02

Piezoelectric effect

Piezoelectric crystals inside the module convert mechanical pressure into pulses of electrical current.

03

Energy storage

An electronic controller rectifies and stores energy in batteries or ultracapacitors integrated into the system.

04

Distribution

Energy powers LED street lighting, security cameras, road signs, or feeds back into the urban grid.

Applications

Where can it be installed?

Any road with steady traffic is a potential energy source.

High traffic

Parking lots

Shopping center and airport entrances with hundreds of vehicles daily. Ideal for self-financing LED lighting.

School zones

In front of schools

The mandatory speed bumps already required generate electricity for traffic lights and signs in school zones.

Infrastructure

Toll booths

Thousands of heavy vehicles daily — one of the most profitable locations for large-scale piezoelectric generation.

Municipalities

Residential streets

Replaces conventional speed bumps with no extra construction. Powers neighborhood streetlights and security cameras.

Industry

Industrial parks

Freight truck traffic generates substantial energy to power warehouse lighting and entry points.

Smart city

Main avenues

Integration with urban management systems: real-time traffic data and energy for EV charging stations.
